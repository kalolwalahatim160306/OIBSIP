Nikola Tesla

1856 - 1943

The Father of Modern Electricity

"The present is theirs; the future, for which I really worked, is mine."

Introduction

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor and electrical engineer. He is admired for his brilliant ideas and his work on electricity. His inventions helped build the modern electrical world that we use every day.

Early Life

Tesla was born on July 10, 1856, in Smiljan, in what is now Croatia. He studied engineering and physics in Europe. In 1884 he moved to New York City and began working on electrical inventions. His talent soon made him one of the most important inventors of his time.

Major Inventions and Discoveries

Tesla created the alternating current system, the Tesla coil, and the induction motor. He also worked on ideas for wireless power. These inventions are still used in homes, factories, and power stations around the world.

Contributions to Alternating Current (AC)

Tesla proved that alternating current could send electricity over long distances better than direct current. With George Westinghouse, he showed AC at the 1893 World's Fair in Chicago. The Niagara Falls power plant later used his system. Today, AC is the standard for power grids worldwide.

Legacy and Impact on Modern Technology

Tesla's work still shapes modern life. Motors, transformers, and the electricity in our homes all trace back to his discoveries. He imagined wireless communication long before radios and smartphones. He remains one of history's greatest inventors.

Timeline

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan.
  • 1884 - Arrived in New York City.
  • 1887 - Developed AC systems and induction motor.
  • 1891 - Invented the Tesla coil.
  • 1893 - Demonstrated AC at the Chicago World's Fair.
  • 1895 - Niagara Falls plant used his AC system.
  • 1943 - Died in New York City.
